MALIK UL MULK - THE OWNER OF SOVEREIGNTY

Allah is Malik ul Mulk, the Owner of Sovereignty. In other words, Allah is the real King. We have to look at the Quran from two different references. First the reference to Al Malik:

113 Thus have we sent this down an Arabic Quran and explained in there in detail some of the warnings in order that they may fear Allah or that it may cause their remembrance (of Him).
114 Then exalted be Allah the real King! Do not hasten with the Quran before its revelation to you is completed but say "My Rabb (Lord)! Advance me in knowledge."

[Quran: Ta Ha, Chapter 20]

Next we have to look at the reference to Mulk:

1 Blessed is He in whose hand is the Sovereignty, and He is able to do all things

[Quran: Al Mulk, Chapter 67]

In the previous chapter we were told that Allah is the Majestic. Now we come to the King - Al Malik. A king is only a king if there is kingdom. Allah is the King and everything in existence, visible and invisible is His kingdom. Since everything is in Allah's kingdom, He is the owner of everything and therefore Allah is able to do whatever He likes in His kingdom.

Therefore, even the Quran belongs in His kingdom. Allah sent the Quran in Arabic because that was His decision. We must learn to recite the Quran in Arabic because that is the language chosen by Allah, and that is the language that Allah preferred over all other languages. Then we are told: Do not hasten with the Quran before its revelation to you is completed but say "My Rabb (Lord)! Advance me in knowledge."

The Quran belongs to Allah. We belong to Allah. Therefore, Allah took the responsibility of completing the revelation of the Quran to Muhammad . That is what it means to be a true King. Allah the true King takes on the responsibility of everything in His Kingdom. Allah the true King takes the responsibility of everything for all His creation. Some of the responsibilities are of feeding, watering, physical growth and intellectual development. No one besides Allah is capable of providing all that. The other side of this is that Allah does that without expecting anything in return! In the case of an ordinary king, the king expects some sort of taxes and loyalty for looking after his subjects. Disloyalty to a human king is a crime punishable by banishment from the kingdom, or imprisonment. But Allah, the true King, looks after all creation regardless of their loyalty to Him and He is aware that the creation cannot leave His Kingdom and go anywhere! The loyalty will only be questioned on the Day of Judgement. But while we are in this life, Allah provides for us without questioning our faith, regardless of our colour, race or creed. Therefore the only true King is Allah and none other.

It is short sightedness on the part of disbelievers that they do not believe in Allah. Since Allah is fair towards all His creation in this world, the disbelievers find no reason to believe in Allah. All their needs are provided for even when they disbelieve. They do not realise that Allah is patient and fair to His subjects. But Allah did send His Messengers to warn the people of what awaits them in the next world if they do not believe in Allah. And Allah states clearly in the Quran:

115 "Did you then think that We had created you in jest and that you would not return back to Us (for account)?"
116 Therefore exalted be Allah the real King! There is no god only He the Rabb (Lord) of the throne of honour.

[Quran: Al Muminoon, Chapter 23]

When we deal with each other we are polite to each other and say: "Thank you!" over every little thing. Good manners do not cost anything. Then why is it so difficult to say thanks to Allah the true King who has given us countless blessings? We only have to move our tongue and say Al Hamdu Lillah - All praise is for Allah. It does not cost us anything to say that. Just by moving the tongue, Allah will be lenient on us on the Day of Judgement, Insha Allah.

When people approach an ordinary king, they are courteous. But when it comes to showing good manners before Allah, they are insolent. Even in the animals created by Allah there are examples for us to follow. Every animal, when it comes to drinking water, bows its head before Allah. We should learn from that. There is nothing wrong in bowing our head to Allah especially when He is the true King who does not need us, yet we need Him.

Only Allah is the true Malik ul Mulk Zul Jalali wal Ikram - Allah is the Owner of Sovereignty, possessor of Majesty and Bounty.

Once Muhammad heard someone praying with the following words:

Allahumma Inni Asaluka Bismik al Azeem il Azam il Hannan il Mannani Malik ul Mulki Zul Jalali wal Ikram

Muhammad said, that person had prayed to Allah with Ism Azam - the Greatest Name. The person who prays to Allah, calling upon Allah with that Name, his prayers will be accepted. Whatever that person asks for from Allah, he will receive.

The real true Malik - King is none other than Allah who provides for all the creation for all their needs. The only requirement for this provision from the true King is that we move our Lisaan - tongue and thank and praise Him for all His Karam - generosity which He bestows on every living thing. Al Hamdu Lillah - All praise is for Allah who guided us through the blessed Lisaan - tongue of His beloved Muhammad . The words that were repeated by Muhammad were recorded in the in the form of the Quran which has been given to us as a gift from Allah. And that Book starts with the letters Alif Laam Meem. Al Hamdu Lillah - All praise is for Allah. La ilaha illa Huwa Rabb ul Arshil Kareem - There is no god only He the Rabb (Lord) of the throne of honour, the One who sent Rasool al Kareem who left us the miracle of Quran al Kareem. Wal Hamdu Lillahi Rabbil Alameen.

MEDITATION

The person who reads Ya Malik ul Mulk1111 times everyday, Allah will make that person self sufficient, Insha Allah.

The person who reads Ya Malik ul Mulk Zul Jalali Wal Ikram 1312 times every night will attain spiritual knowledge, Insha Allah. Furthermore that person will be protected from the cruelty and injustice of others towards him / her, since Allah will take the responsibility of looking after that person.

Remember to recite any Darood of your choice 11 times before and after the above Zikr and send the Hadiya (reward) for the Darood to Allah's Rasool, our Master, Sayyidina Muhammad.
