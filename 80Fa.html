FA - THE ARABIC LETTER

INTRODUCTION

The Arabic Letter Fa () is the 20th Letter in the normal Arabic character set. In the numerical character set, Abjad, Fa () has the value of 80. The position of the Letter Fa () in the Abjad format is 17. This article is about the spiritual meaning of the Letter Fa ().

 

THE LETTER FA

5 So with every difficulty there is relief
6 With every difficulty there is relief.
7 Therefore when you are relieved still work hard
8 And strive to please your Rabb (Lord).

[Quran: Alam Nashrah Chapter 94]

FA INNA MA AL USRI YUSRA(N) INNA MA AL USRI YUSRA

With every difficulty there is relief, with every difficulty there is relief.

The Letter Fa () is for Opening. The Quran has an Opening. And that Opening in the Quran is Al Fatihah Chapter 1.

FATIHAH - OPENING

We are not just required 'to open' Fatah the Quran, but we are also required to read the Quran. And where do we start ? At the Opening Chapter Al Fatihah. Fatihah means Opening. Opening the Quran is the first Opening. Reciting Al Fatihah is the second Opening. Then there is the third opening. That third Opening is the inner 'eye'. Which is the understanding. What is understanding ? To understand is Fahm . Which is Fa, Ha and Meem ().

It is not a coincidence that there are 7 Verses in Al Fatihah - THE OPENING. That is the same number of OPENINGS in the human head. That is the connection between Al Fatihah and our understanding.

Another way to look at it is:

= 889 = Ta (= 400) + Ha (= 8) + Ta (= 400) + Alif (= 1) + Fa ( = 80)

Reducing 889 to a single figure = 8 + 8 + 9 = 25, which then reduces to 2 + 5 = 7 OFTEN REPEATED VERSES.

Similarly with the Salah - Prayer. In Salah we need to recite Al Fatihah the Opening Chapter, in every Rakah. Salah is not Salah without recitation of Al Fatihah. And Al Fatihah is one of the doors of Mercy.

2 That which Allah opens unto mankind of mercy none can withhold it; and that which He withholds none can release thereafter. He is the Mighty, the Wise.

[Quran: Fatir Chapter 35]

Rasool Allah said, "The Um (substance) of the Quran is the seven often repeated verses (Al-Mathani) and is the Great Quran (i.e. Surat-al-Fatihah)."

[Sahih Bukhari]

The door that opens the Mercy of Allah brings relief to every difficulty. No one can open that door of Mercy other than Allah. How does Allah open the door of Mercy ?

 

OPENING THE DOOR OF MERCY

The clue is in the above reference Chapter 35 Verse 2 Ma Yaftahillahu Lin Nasi Mir Rahmatin. To open any door we need a key. What is a Key ? A Key in Arabic is Miftah. Miftah or Key is basically the word Fatah with the Letter Meem () attached at the front. Therefore Opening the door of Mercy is with the Letter Meem (). Next we have to consider what is pleasing to Allah ? The name of His Beloved Muhammad :

107 WA MA ARSALNAKA ILLA RAHMATIL LIL AALAMEEN

107 We Sent you as a Mercy to all the worlds

[Quran: Al Anbiyaa Chapter 21] .

Muhammad is the Key that Opens the Door of Allah's Mercy. How ?

56 INN ALLAHA WA MALAI KATAHU YU SALLU NA ALAN NABIYY YA AYUHAL LAZEENA AMANU SALLU ALAIHI WA SALLIMU TASLIMA

56 Allah and His angels shower blessings on the Nabee (Prophet). You who believe! Ask blessings on him and salute him with a worthy salutation.

[Quran: Al Ahzab Chapter 33]

And if we look at Chapter 48 in the Quran Al Fath - THE VICTORY we find the words : MUHAMMADUR RASOOL ALLAH (MUHAMMAD IS THE MESSENGER OF ALLAH).

 

POVERTY AND REFLECTION

There is Faqr - Poverty and there is Fikkr - Reflection. Fikkr is to reflect on the creation that Allah has brought about. Everything in the universe is created to follow what has been destined for that created thing. Nothing moves without Allah's Knowledge.

The difference between the two words Faqr and Fikkr is the Letter Qaf () in Faqr and the Letter Kaaf () in Fikkr. Therefore the difference between the two words numerically is Qaf (= 100) - Kaaf (= 20) or 100 - 20 = 80 = Fa (). The Letter Fa () is the beginning of the word Fana. Fikkr Reflection leads to FaqrPoverty. But this kind of Fikkr Reflection leads to another kind of Faqr Poverty. This other kind of Faqr Poverty is to become indifferent to, or detachment from, everything in creation and annihilate oneself in Allah.

 

FANA - ANNIHILATION

Fana is Annihilation. Fana is detachment from creation. Fana is diving into the Noon () which is the light and crossing that light to reach Alif (). Another way to look at Fana is from the point of Alif ().

The Letter Alif () when written as a word ALIF is Alif () Laam () and Fa () = (). Fana is journey to Alif () within ALIF (). The analogy is that of a journey of one bubble in a Fulk - Ship.

41 And a Sign for them is that We carried their race in the loaded Fulk (Ark).
42 And We have created for them similar (vessels) on which they ride.

[Quran: Ya Seen Chapter 36]

The human body in this instance is like Fulk () - Ship. The spirit rides 'over' it. That bubble disappears in the Laam () which is the LA MAKAN or LA MANZIL meaning without a house, or homeless. And eventually the bubble merges into the ocean of Ahadiyyat () - Oneness. Most people die when the predestined time of their death arrives in the future. Only the select few die before that time and that death is not in the future but in the past.

Although the body is still 'living' their spirit journeys backwards through the point of non attachment La Makan to Alif () where the thought of their existence has not even occurred. This is the point of Oneness, and since it is the point in time before their physical body came into existence, they see through Allah. They hear through Allah. They speak through Allah. Past, present and future is that very point. This is the point where the person does not exist, Only Allah Exists. And this is what is meant by the following Verse:

17 It is not you who slew them; it was Allah: when you threw (a handful of dust) it was not your act but Allah's: in order that He might test the believers by a gracious trial from Himself: for Allah is He who hears and knows (all things)

[Quran: Al Anfal Chapter 8]

That handful of dust was thrown in a state of Fana, therefore it was not the act of Muhammad but Allah's. To show the connection between the Letter Fa () the above Verse number is 17. The Letter Fa () is the 17 Letter in the Abjad format. And 17 = 1 + 7 = 8, the zero '0' has disappeared from 80. Similarly, even the Chapter number is 8 !

Rasool Allah said: I saw Allah and there was nothing beside Him. A person heard this and asked Ali ibn Talib () about the meaning of this statement. Ali ibn Talib () replied: This moment is that very moment. Ali ibn Talib () was in the state of Fana at that time.
