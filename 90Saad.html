SAAD - THE ARABIC LETTER

INTRODUCTION

The Arabic Letter Saad () is one of the Muqattaat Letters. That is it is used as a Letter at the start of a Chapter in the Quran. The position of the Letter Saad () in the normal Arabic character set is 14. In the numerical character set, Abjad, Saad () has the value of 90. The position of the Letter Saad () in Abjad format is 18. This article is about the spiritual meaning of the Letter Saad ().

 

THE LETTER SAAD

The Letter Saad () is used as a Muqattaat Letter in the Quran three times. The first usage is as follows:

1 Alif Laam Meem Saad.
2 A Book revealed to you so let there be no heaviness in your heart by any difficulty on that account that with it you may warn (the erring) and a reminder to the believers.

[Quran: Al Aaraf Chapter 7]

From the above reference we can see that the Letter Saad () is used for Sadr meaning 'heart, in the above verse' or Chest.

We have all heard the expression : "Get it off your chest !" Meaning "What is your problem ?" Meaning "Tell me your problem."

 

SADR - CHEST

1 Have We not expanded your Chest?

[Quran: Alam Nashrah Chapter 94]

But, Allah Knows the secrets we hide in our hearts or in the Sadr or chest. Therefore Allah did not need to ask, but He revealed A BOOK (Chapter 7 Verse 2) so that there would be no heaviness in the heart of His Beloved, Muhammad . What was causing the heaviness ? The difficulty of turning a nation of idol worshippers to Muslims. The difficulty of explaining that idol worshipping is a grave mistake which will be rewarded by punishment in hell. The difficulty of spreading the Message of Allah to all the nations since there would be no more Messengers of Allah after Muhammad. The difficulty of 'showing' the people that Al Sirat al Mustaqeem THE PATH THAT IS STRAIGHT is the best way.

Not only that, being a Messenger of Allah was not an easy task for any of them. There is no running away. Remember Yunus () whose title is Zaan-Noon?

87 And remember Zaan-noon when he departed in wrath: he imagined that We had no power over him! But he cried through the depths of darkness "There is no god only You: Glory to You: I was indeed wrong!"

[Quran: Al Anbiyaa Chapter 21]

Therefore, Allah revealed A BOOK to overcome this difficulty. What was that Book ? The Quran would have been THE BOOK (Al Kitab) not A BOOK (Kitabun). The 'BOOK' in question is the Letters ALIF LAAM MEEM SAAD

What turns a Nafs Ammarah the reckless soul to the Nafs Lawwamah the restless soul ? And what turns the Nafs Lawwamah to Nafs Mutmainnah the PEACEFUL soul ? It is Sabr Patience.

 

SABR - PATIENCE

Sabr is a quality of endurance. Sabr is a quality where a person is not easily provoked. Sabr is a quality where a person is not hasty.

45 Seek help in patience and prayer; and truly it is hard save for the humble-minded,
46 Who know that they will have to meet their Rabb (Lord), and that to Him they are returning.

[Quran: Al Baqara Chapter 2]

The thing about Sabr is that it comes with time. Very few are born with it. As we have all observed that a baby that is hungry starts crying. When the baby grows up to be a child, Sawm Fast or Siyyam Fasting becomes necessary. Why ?

Rasool Allah said that your Rabb (Lord) said, "Every (sinful) deed can be expiated; and the fast is for Me, so I will give the reward for it; and the smell which comes out of the mouth of a fasting person, is better in Allah's Sight than the smell of musk."

[Sahih Bukhari]

To break this habit of impatience. Fasting is necessary. Fasting is a means for us to learn Sabr Patience. If the reward for Fasting is with Allah then what about the reward for Sabr ? That is also with Allah.


115 And be steadfast in patience; for truly Allah will not suffer the reward of the righteous to perish.

[Quran: Hud Chapter 11]

But there is catch ! Sabr is only valid in this world not in the next world ! How can that be ?

It is stated in the Quran:

14 (And it is said to them): This is the Fire which you used to deny.
15 Is this magic, or do you not see?
16 Endure the heat thereof, and whether you are patient or impatient it is all one for you. You are only being paid for what you used to do.

[Quran: At Tur Chapter 52]

Since we mentioned fasting, now let us consider fasting.

 

SAWM - FAST

Sawm A Fast, is something very personal. Fasting is a trust between the person who is fasting and Allah. Nobody else. The only thing stopping a fasting person from indulging in that which is prohibited from dawn to dusk is trust. That trust can only be kept by the one who is fasting. Therefore, fasting is for Allah. And the reward is from Allah. Fasting is not just abstaining from food and drink, but the fast also applies to eyes, ears, hands and feet.

There is a very important little anecdote with a lesson in Kashf Al Mahjub by Ali bin Usman al Hujwiri ():

Abu Yazid, when he was entering Rayy on his way from Hijaz, the people of the city ran to meet him in order that they might show him honour. Their attentions distracted him and turned his thoughts away from Allah. When he came to the bazaar, he took a loaf from his sleeve and began to eat. They all departed, for it was the month of Ramadan. He said to his disciple who was travelling with him: "You see! As soon as I perform a single act of Shariat they all reject me."

[Kashf Al Mahjub: On Blame, Chapter 6]

 

THE OTHER TWO MENTIONS OF SAAD

Now Let us go back to the Letter Saad (). It was mentioned at the start of this article that the Letter Saad () is used three times as a Muqattaat Letter. The first use is in Al Aaraf Chapter 7 as shown at the top. The other two occurrences are as follows:

1 Kaaf Ha Ya Ayn Saad.
2 (This is) a recital of Mercy of your Rabb to His Servant Zakariya.

[Quran: Maryam Chapter 19]

1 Saad. By the Quran which contains the reminder.

[Quran: Saad Chapter 38]

The interesting point about all these three references where the Letter Saad () is used as a Muqattaat Letter is that the word Zikr appears. The Letter Saad () is connected with the Letter Zal () .

The Letter Saad () is connected to the Zikr (). But which Zikr ? Remember Zaan-Noon, Yunus () ?

What was the Zikr of Zaan-Noon ?

LA ILAHA ILLA ANTA SUBHANA KA INNI KUNTU MINAZ ZALIMEEN

87 And remember Zaan-noon when he departed in wrath: he imagined that We had no power over him! But he cried through the depths of darkness "There is no god only You: Glory to You: I was indeed wrong!"

[Quran:Al Anbiyaa Chapter 21]

What do we call this Verse ? Ayat KAREEM . Where have we seen the Letter Kaaf () before ? The Letter Kaaf () is only used once as a Muqattaat Letter in Chapter 19 (Maryam) Verse 1 where the Letter Saad () completes these 5 Letters.

To show the connection between Letter Saad (), Zaan-Noon and the above five Letters () we find in the Quran: Chapter 21, Verse 89 mentions Zakariya (), and if we still cannot see the connection, Allah gives us two more clues in Verse 91.

87 And remember Zaan-noon when he departed in wrath: he imagined that We had no power over him! But he cried through the depths of darkness "There is no god only You: Glory to You: I was indeed wrong!"
88 So We listened to him: and delivered him from distress: and thus do We deliver those who have faith.
89 And (remember) Zakariya when he cried to his Rabb (Lord): "My Rabb (Lord)! leave me not without offspring though You are the best of inheritors.
90 So We listened to him: and We granted him Yahya: We cured his wife's (barrenness) for him. These three were ever quick in emulation in good works: they used to call on Us with love and reverence and humble themselves before Us.
91 And (remember) her who guarded her chastity: We breathed into her of Our Spirit and We made her and her son a Sign for all peoples.

[Quran: Al Anbiyaa Chapter 21]

Notice Verse 91 the final clue, is 19 backwards. That is the second clue. And what is the name of Chapter 19 ? Maryam (). How does Chapter Maryam start ?

 

CONCLUSION

Yunus () was in the belly of the fish for 3 days and 3 nights. The Letter Saad () is used as a Muqattaat Letter 3 times.

115 And be steadfast in patience; for truly Allah will not suffer the reward of the righteous to perish.

[Quran: Hud Chapter 11]

Yunus () received the reward in 3 days and 3 nights. And we must all remember the following concerning Yunus ():

Rasool Allah said, "None should say that I am better than Yunus bin Matta."

[Sahih Bukhari]

In our case it takes longer. None of us is innocent as the Messengers of Allah (). Therefore in our case it takes longer, so we must not give up hope. We must be patient. Insha Allah, Allah will not hold back the reward for patience. With patience we must do Zikr Allah. Which Zikr ? How about Ayat Kareem ? Or how about Allahu Samad [Chapter 112 Verse 2]? Sadr is the place of Knowledge. It is where one comprehends. Impatience stems from not knowing. Sabr is achieved by comprehending.

May Allah teach us all Patience and have Mercy on us all. May Allah make us comprehend. May Allah reward our Patience. Ameen.

Only Allah Knows Best.
