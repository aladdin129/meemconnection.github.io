BISMILLAH HIR RAHMAN NIR RAHEEM

SHAYTAN'S FATE ?

First of all who or what is shaytan ? Look at the Arabic word and Shayteen and that is the clue. SHAY is a "THING". TEEN is what we humans are made of, which is CLAY.

Therefore Shaytan is a "thing within the clay". It is part of the human constitution. It is like a fire which keeps the body warm when alive. On death Shaytan leaves and the lifeless body becomes cold. Therefore Shaytan is only doing what it was supposed to do. To keep the blood flowing in the body. Just like it is stated in a Hadees, Shaytan runs in the human body like blood circulates. What does that mean ? Exactly what it says. It flows as blood in the human body. Another analogy of the human body is the planet earth. On the surfcae it is cool or warm or hot. In the core of the Earth there is a raging fire which we see erupting as volcanoes.

Ask any spiritual master WHAT is Shaytan, the honest and true answer will be GHAIR ALLAH. Ghair Allah means other than Allah. Now if we consider the verses of the Quran and we are told time and time again, Whatever is heaven and earth belongs to Allah. Whatever is in heaven and earth declares the praises of Allah. Therefore we can safely deduce that if everything in heaven and earth belongs to Allah, then how can Shaytan exist ? And all the spiritual masters will agree in there hearts that Shaytan is other than Allah, therefore it does not exist. All the affectations of the heart are from Allah. Whether good or evil. Hence the statements in the Quran to the effect: He only guides whom He wants to. And He leaves others to stray. Furthermore: It is your duty to pass the Message, it is up to Us whom We wish to guide.

So coming back to the question SHAYTAN'S FATE ? The answer is nothing will happen to Shaytan. How can Shaytan have a fate, if it does not exist? It is other than Allah. The only existence is that of Allah. Hence the utterance by Rasool Allah "I saw Allah and there was nothing beside Him" Hadrat Ali () confirmed "This moment is that very moment."

Allah is, as He was and there is nothing beside Him.

Darood (Blessings) and Salaam (Peace) on Muhammad, His Family, and His Companions

Khalid M. Malik
